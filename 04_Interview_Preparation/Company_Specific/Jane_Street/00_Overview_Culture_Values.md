# Jane Street Interview Preparation

## Company Overview

**Type:** Proprietary trading firm (market maker)  
**Founded:** 2000  
**HQ:** New York  
**Size:** ~2,000 employees  
**AUM:** ~$20+ billion

## Culture & Values

- **Collaborative**: Work closely with others, share ideas openly
- **Intellectual curiosity**: Love of learning and problem-solving
- **Low ego**: Best idea wins, regardless of seniority
- **Risk-aware**: Quantify and manage risk carefully
- **Ethical**: Integrity in all dealings

## Interview Process

### Typical Flow
1. Online assessment / HackerRank (1 hour)
2. Phone screen with trader/researcher (45-60 min)
3. Superday / Virtual Day (4-6 hours)
   - Multiple technical rounds
   - Trading game / simulation
   - Probability & expected value
   - Coding challenge

### What They're Looking For
- Sharp quantitative thinking
- Clear communication of thought process
- Ability to make decisions with incomplete information
- Intellectual humility
- Genuine curiosity about markets

## Key Focus Areas

| Area | Weight | Preparation |
|------|--------|-------------|
| Probability & Expected Value | 35% | Daily mental math practice |
| Trading/Market Making | 25% | Trading games, Kelly criterion |
| ML/Statistics | 20% | Algorithm deep dives |
| Coding | 20% | Clean, correct solutions |

### 1. Probability (CRITICAL)

### 2. Trading Games
- Market making simulations
- Information asymmetry

### 3. Bayesian Inference
- Prior/posterior updating

### 4. Kelly Criterion
- Optimal bet sizing
- Fractional Kelly

## Tips for Success

1. **Think out loud**: They care about process, not just answers
2. **Ask clarifying questions**: Shows thoughtfulness
3. **Start simple**: Build up from basic cases
4. **Quantify uncertainty**: "I'm about 70% confident..."
5. **Admit when stuck**: Better than pretending to know
6. **Show genuine interest**: Ask good questions about their work
